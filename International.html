Al Qaeda's no. 2 leader killed by Israeli agents in Iran: New York Times

Abu Muhammad al-Masri, al Qaeda's second-in-command, was killed in Iran in August by Israeli operatives acting at US' behest, reported New York Times citing intelligence officials. He's accused of being one of the masterminds of the 1998 bombings of two US embassies in Africa. He was killed along with his daughter, Miriam, the widow of Osama bin Laden's son Hamza.


New Year fireworks banned in Netherlands over COVID-19 fears

Netherlands on Friday said it's banning the sale and bursting of fireworks over New Year amid the coronavirus pandemic. The ban is to "avoid extra pressure on already overburdened care workers and to maintain public order", it added. People who violate the ban would be liable for $118 fine and a criminal record, Dutch Justice Minister Ferd Grapperhaus said.


Pope Francis congratulates Joe Biden on US presidential election win

Pope Francis offered his "blessings and congratulations" to Joe Biden on his US presidential election victory during a phone call on Thursday, the Democrat's transition team said. Biden has expressed his hopes to work with Francis on issues such as climate change, poverty and immigration, it added. Biden will become the second Catholic president in US history, when sworn in.


US records biggest one-day jump in COVID-19 cases at over 1.77L

The US on Friday recorded its biggest one-day rise in coronavirus cases as over 1.77 lakh people tested positive, The Washington Post reported. At least ten states in the country reported a record daily jump in COVID-19 cases. The US is the worst coronavirus-hit country in the world with nearly 1.08 crore COVID-19 cases and over 2.4 lakh deaths.
